1. Indefinido vs. Imperfecto

Imperfecto (The Background)

"Was ...-ing", "Used to", Description.

  • Setting the scene (weather, time, age).
  • Action in progress (interrupted).
  • Habits in the past.

Indefinido (The Action)

Completed action, Specific time.

  • The main event or plot.
  • Action that interrupts.
  • Specific start/end time.

The "Interruption" Rule:

While something was happening (Imperfect), something happened (Indefinido).

Example: Yo dormía (was sleeping) cuando el teléfono sonó (rang).

Text Type: Diary (Diario Íntimo)

A private record of thoughts and feelings. It often uses a mix of past tenses.

Key Features:

  • Date: Essential at the top.
  • Opening: "Querido diario:" (Dear diary).
  • Tone: Very personal, emotional, rhetorical questions.
  • Closing: Usually brief ("Hasta mañana", "Buenas noches").
