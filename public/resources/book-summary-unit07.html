4. Grammar: Hay vs. Estar

Location & Existence

HAY (Existence)

Use it to say something exists (There is / There are).

Rule: Use with indefinite articles (un/una) or numbers.

En mi cuarto hay una cama.

ESTAR (Location)

Use it to locate a SPECIFIC thing.

Rule: Use with definite articles (el/la) or names.

La cama está en el cuarto.

7. Grammar: Giving Directions (Imperative)

To give orders or directions to a friend (informal), use the Tú form of the Imperative.

Common Direction Commands:

Cruzar (To cross)
¡Cruza!
Doblar / Girar (To turn)
¡Dobla! / ¡Gira!
Seguir (To continue/follow)
¡Sigue recto!
Ir (Irregular)
¡Ve!
