Unit 2: Adjectives & Pronouns

Agreement, Subject Pronouns, and Possessives

1. Adjective Agreement (Concordancia)

Adjectives describe nouns (people or things). In Spanish, they must match the noun in:
1. Gender (Masculine/Feminine)
2. Number (Singular/Plural)

Example: El chico alto (The tall boy) vs. Las chicas altas (The tall girls).

2. Subject Pronouns

Singular

  • Yo (I)
  • Tú (You - informal)
  • Él / Ella (He / She)
  • Usted (You - formal)

Plural

  • Nosotros / Nosotras (We)
  • Vosotros / Vosotras (You all - Spain informal)
  • Ellos / Ellas (They)
  • Ustedes (You all - Latin Am. / Formal Spain)

3. Possessive Adjectives (My, Your, His...)

Possessives show ownership. They agree in number (singular/plural) with the thing possessed, not the owner.

Mi / Mis (My)
Tu / Tus (Your)
Su / Sus (His/Her/Their)
Nuestro/a/os/as (Our)
