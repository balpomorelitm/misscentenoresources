3. Grammar: The Verb Doler (To hurt)

Structure: Like GUSTAR

The verb Doler (o>ue) does not follow regular rules. It changes according to what hurts (singular or plural), not the person who feels it.

Singular Pain

Me duele la cabeza.

Le duele la espalda.

Plural Pain

Me duelen los pies.

Te duelen los ojos.

Wait! Note that we use "la cabeza" (the head) instead of "mi cabeza" (my head) in Spanish when talking about body parts.

5. At the Doctor (En la Consulta)

Common phrases used during a medical visit.

Doctor:

¿Qué le pasa? / ¿Dónde le duele?

Paciente:

No me siento bien. / Tengo dolor de cabeza.

Doctor's advice:

  • Tome esta medicina.
  • Descanse por tres días.
  • Beba más agua.

7. Grammar: Giving Advice (Imperative)

When a doctor gives advice, they use the **Formal Imperative (Usted)**. Here is how to form it:

Verb Ending Formal Imperative (Usted) Example
-AR -e Tome
-ER / -IR -a Beba
